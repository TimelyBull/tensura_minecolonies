# Roadmap

Features that are **not yet implemented**. Everything here is planned or
under consideration — none of it is in the mod today.

!!! tip "What already works"
    Everything in the [Features](features/diplomacy.md) section is in the game
    now. This page covers only unbuilt work.

Two categories:

- **:material-ruler-square: Designed, not yet built** — a clear, decided
  shape; waiting on a build pass.
- **:material-lightbulb-outline: Ideas & maybes** — uncertain scope, not
  committed; may change or never ship.

---

## :material-ruler-square: Designed, not yet built

### Sieges (retaliation raids)

[Declaring war](features/rival-colonies.md) on a faction you were allied with
already shatters the relationship and scales up that settlement's garrison.
The siege is the unbuilt follow-up: the betrayed faction launches a raid on
*your* colony, larger than a normal raid (possibly multi-night, possibly with
a lead boss), scaled by the broken tier.

> *Status: designed; the betrayal trigger exists, the siege does not.*

### More boss lore events

One lore event exists — the Orc Disaster, a horde Clayman sends at a colony
that wronged him. Two more are designed:

- **Charybdis** — a flying set-piece encounter rather than a ground horde.
- **Ifrit** — a single named-boss fight rather than a wave.

> *Status: designed as a set; one built, the rest pending.*

### Lending named monsters

[Citizen lending](features/diplomacy.md) currently accepts ordinary
colonists only. The extension would allow lending your named,
identity-carrying monsters as well.

> *Status: designed; requires keeping a lent monster's identity intact.*

### Faction-settlement quests

A planned class of [diplomacy](features/diplomacy.md) deal tied to
[rival settlements](features/rival-colonies.md): visit a faction's town,
build at it, or help defend it. Depends on settlements having more content
first.

> *Status: designed; waiting on deeper settlement content.*

### War-party convenience

When you [declare war](features/rival-colonies.md), the war party is taken
from monsters currently at your side. A planned option would summon absent
subordinates into the party first.

> *Status: small, designed.*

---

## :material-lightbulb-outline: Ideas & maybes

### Player-vs-player colony raiding

The conquest loop could be turned against another player's colony in
multiplayer. Unresolved questions about consent, fairness, and safety keep
this uncommitted.

> *Status: idea only — uncertain, unscoped.*

---

## :material-tune: Ongoing: balance

Combat values — rival garrison strength, betrayal scaling, raid difficulty —
are first-draft and not yet playtested. Expect changes.
