# Roadmap

This page is an honest look at **what's planned but not yet in the mod**.
Everything below is **NOT YET IMPLEMENTED** — it's here so you know where
things are heading, not what you can do today.

!!! tip "Looking for what already works?"
    Everything in the [Features](features/diplomacy.md) section is in the
    game now. This page is only about the *future*.

The planned work falls into two honest buckets:

- **:material-ruler-square: Designed, not yet built** — features that are
  fully thought through and have a clear shape; they're waiting on a build
  pass, not on a decision.
- **:material-lightbulb-outline: Ideas & maybes** — directions we're
  interested in but haven't committed to; the scope is still fuzzy and they
  may change a lot or never happen.

---

## :material-ruler-square: Designed, not yet built

These are planned with a clear idea of what they'd be.

### Sieges — when a betrayed ally strikes back

Today, [betraying an ally](features/rival-colonies.md) (declaring war on a
faction you had a pact or covenant with) shatters the relationship and makes
that settlement's defenders fight harder. The **siege** is the other half of
that story, not yet built: a faction you betrayed marches a **retaliatory
super-raid** on *your* colony — bigger than a normal raid, possibly led by a
boss with an elite guard, possibly over multiple nights. The deeper the bond
you broke, the worse the reckoning.

> *Status: designed. The betrayal that triggers it already exists; the
> answering siege does not.*

### More boss lore events

Right now there's one full **lore event** — the Orc Disaster, Clayman's
horde marching on a colony that wronged him. Two more are designed in the
same mold but not yet built:

- **Charybdis** — a flying set-piece catastrophe rather than a ground horde.
- **Ifrit** — a named-boss duel, a single dangerous confrontation instead of
  a wave.

More events in this style are intended over time; each is a bespoke
encounter tied to a particular Tensura power.

> *Status: designed as a family; one built, the rest pending.*

### Lending your named monsters

[Citizen lending](features/diplomacy.md) currently works with your ordinary
colonists. Lending your **named, identity-carrying monsters** (the ones you
move between your side and the colony) is a planned extension so your
signature characters can be sent on training loans too.

> *Status: designed; needs care so a lent character keeps its identity.*

### Faction-settlement quests

Once [rival colonies](features/rival-colonies.md) are fully fleshed out,
diplomacy is meant to gain a new class of deal that uses them: *"visit the
faction's town,"* *"build something at their settlement,"* *"help defend
their holdings,"* and similar territory-flavoured tasks. This needs the
settlement world to be a richer place first.

> *Status: designed; waiting on deeper settlement content.*

### War-party quality-of-life

When you [declare war](features/rival-colonies.md), your war party is drawn
from the monsters currently at your side. A planned convenience would let you
**summon absent subordinates into the party first**, so you aren't limited to
whoever happens to be standing near you when you march.

> *Status: small, designed polish.*

## :material-lightbulb-outline: Ideas & maybes

Directions we're interested in but haven't committed to. Scope is uncertain
and these may change drastically — or never arrive.

### Player-vs-player colony raiding

The conquest loop you use against generated settlements could, in principle,
be turned against **another player's colony** on a multiplayer server. This
is a genuine *maybe*: it raises real questions about consent, fairness, and
safety that haven't been answered, so it's firmly in the "someday, perhaps"
column rather than the plan.

> *Status: idea only — uncertain, unscoped.*

---

## :material-tune: Ongoing: balance & polish

Many of the mod's combat numbers — how tough rival garrisons are, how much a
betrayal scales them up, raid difficulty — are **first-draft values** set
before extensive playtesting. Expect them to be tuned over time as the
systems get played. If something feels too easy or too brutal, that's the
kind of thing a balance pass is meant to catch.

---

!!! note "This list will change"
    Plans shift as the mod grows. Nothing here is a promise of a date, an
    order, or that a feature will land exactly as described — it's a snapshot
    of current intent.
