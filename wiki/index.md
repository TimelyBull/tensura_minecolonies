# Tensura × MineColonies

> Build a colony. Raise reincarnated monsters as its people. Win the world over — or conquer it.

This mod bridges two of Minecraft's biggest worlds: the reincarnated-monster
fantasy of **Tensura: Reincarnated** and the colony-builder of
**MineColonies**. Your goblins, orcs, dwarves and lizardmen stop being wild
mobs and become *colonists* — and the bosses, kingdoms and demon lords of
the Tensura world become powers you can befriend, trade with, or go to war
against.

!!! note "Who this guide is for"
    These pages explain what each feature **does** and **how to play with
    it** — not how it's built. If you just installed the mod and want to
    know what you can do, you're in the right place. Start with
    [Getting Started](getting-started.md).

## The core idea: two bodies, one identity

The heart of the mod is a simple, powerful trick: **one named monster can
live two lives.**

- **At your side** it's a full Tensura creature — it fights, evolves, has
  Existence Points (EP) and skills, and follows your commands.
- **In your colony** it's a MineColonies citizen — it takes a job, levels
  up work skills, lives in a house, and contributes to your town.

You move a named monster between these two roles whenever you like
(at a small magicule cost). Send your veteran fighter home to work the
forge for a while; summon a skilled crafter back to your side for a fight.
It keeps **one identity** the whole time — the same name, the same
progress, the same creature — just wearing a different hat.

This single bridge is what everything else is built on.

## Two paths through the world

Once your colony is on its feet, the great factions of the Tensura world
react to you — and you choose how to deal with them:

| | The Diplomat | The Warlord |
|---|---|---|
| **How** | Open relations, complete their deals | Discover their settlement, declare war |
| **Climb** | Diplomacy → Alliance → Covenant | Win the assault (kill the boss + their garrison) |
| **Rewards** | Buffs, trade caravans, gifts, sworn-skill | Citizens, the faction's signature skill, loot |
| **Risk** | Slow; relations can decay or shatter | A hard fight; betraying an ally makes it harder |

Both paths are optional, and the whole faction layer can be switched off in
the [config](reference/config.md) if you'd rather just build in peace.
